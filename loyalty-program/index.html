Patient Loyalty Program — LATAM

A one-time acquisition reward for patients who save a card on file: book online, save a card, pay with it — earn a credit you can spend on a future visit. Mexico and Brazil at launch.

Value Proposition

Why we are building this

In LATAM, a large share of patients book online but do not save a card on file — so their next visit defaults to "pay at the office", losing the booking commitment and the payments-economics lift that comes with online payment. This program rewards patients for crossing that bridge: save a card, pay online, earn a credit you can spend on a future visit.

Wallet adoption

The qualifying action — saving a card during a booking — is what we are paying for. Each grant converts a "no-card-on-file" patient into a "card-on-file" patient, permanently.

Higher online-paid share

Patients with a card on file are more likely to pay online on their next visit. The credit becomes spendable on online-paid visits only — so redemption itself reinforces the online-paid behaviour we want to grow.

Short-term retention

A six-month expiry on the credit creates a tangible reason to come back. Rebooking inside the credit's lifetime is the primary retention metric for the experiment.

Doctor invisible — zero adoption friction

The program runs entirely on the patient side. Doctors do not opt in, are not invoiced, and see no change in their payouts, dashboards, or tax documents. No per-merchant rollout, no enablement work — it just turns on for eligible patients.

Tax-clean by design

DocPlanner funds the reward (not the doctor). The doctor still issues a CFDI / NFS-e for the full visit price and is paid the full visit price — identical to a non-loyalty visit. The program is recognised as a DP-side marketing/loyalty expense, deductible against a periodic invoice from the payment processor. No doctor-side B2B subsidy invoice, no IVA exposure for IVA-exempt medical specialists.

How It Works

Two coupled sub-cycles ship behind a single shared feature flag. Collection handles earning the credit; Redemption handles spending it. The patient-visible launch only happens once both sub-cycles are live and the dLocal funds-flow sandbox tests have passed.

In plain English

  1. A logged-in patient books with a Mexican or Brazilian doctor on DP Payments via dLocal.
  2. If the patient has no card on file, is in the experiment's treatment group, has not earned the credit before, and accepts the T&Cs at the card-save step, they become eligible for a one-time grant.
  3. They save the card and pay for the visit with it.
  4. After the visit ends (upfront) or after the existing BNPL post-visit capture fires, a scheduled job checks the visit. If nothing has been cancelled or refunded by then, the credit lands on their balance.
  5. The credit is the patient's property from that moment. It expires six months later. They are reminded 30 days and 7 days before expiry.
  6. On any future online-paid visit with an MX or BR DP Payments doctor, they can apply part or all of the credit at checkout. The doctor still receives the full visit price — the credit-funded portion is transferred from DocPlanner's platform pool at payout time.

Earning Side (Collection)

Eligibility (server-side)

  • Logged-in patient (guests are out).
  • Doctor is in Mexico or Brazil and is onboarded onto DP Payments via dLocal.
  • The patient has zero cards on file in their wallet at the moment of booking.
  • The patient saves a card during the booking flow and pays for the visit with that newly saved card.
  • The patient has not previously earned the loyalty credit (one-time per patient, surviving "remove card and re-add" attempts).

All conditions are evaluated server-side from the logged-in patient identity and the doctor's market — no client-supplied locale or country signal is trusted.

Terms & conditions at card-save

  • The patient sees the loyalty T&Cs before the card is saved through the qualifying booking flow. Acceptance is recorded with patient identifier and timestamp.
  • T&Cs explicitly disclose: credit is non-cashable, non-transferable between patients, redeemable only on bookings paid through DP Payments, non-safeguarded (not a regulated financial product, no cash value), and expires six months after grant and on patient account closure.
  • The word "cashback" is forbidden on every patient-facing surface — copy uses "credit" / "wallet credit" / "voucher".

Grant timing

Payment typeWhen the grant fires
Upfront-paid visit When visit.endAt < now AND no cancellation or refund has been recorded against the qualifying payment at the moment the grant job evaluates eligibility. Implementation: a scheduled job sweeps visits past their endAt and fires grants. Reschedules push the evaluation moment to the new endAt.
BNPL / deferred / post-visit-capture At the same operational moment as the existing post-visit payment capture for that flow — e.g. the 48-hour cron, or the earlier doctor-marked outcome that closes the payment cycle.
Pre-grant cancel / refund: no grant — but eligibility is preserved

If the qualifying visit is cancelled or fully refunded before the grant moment, no credit is issued. A partial refund before the grant moment also blocks the grant — only fully completed payments qualify. The patient's one-time eligibility is preserved and a future qualifying booking can still earn.

Notifications & visibility

  • After the grant fires, the patient is notified (in-product + email) with the amount and the expiry date.
  • The patient sees their current loyalty balance and the next expiry date on the wallet / payment-methods page.
  • Promotional copy appears at the payment-method-selection step and on the wallet step where the card is saved. The booking success page links the patient to the wallet, where the credit and its expiry will be visible once the grant fires.
  • Patients are reminded 30 days and 7 days before any grant is due to expire, if it is still wholly or partially unspent.

Spending Side (Redemption)

REVPAY-6420 24 ACs · AC-LOYALTY-RED-001..024 L

At checkout

  • On any future online-paid visit with an MX or BR doctor on DP Payments via dLocal, the patient can apply part or all of their loyalty balance to the visit price.
  • If the applied credit covers the visit fully, the card is not charged for that visit; the doctor still receives the full visit price via a Platform→merchant top-up.
  • If the applied credit covers only part of the price, the remainder is charged to the patient's saved card and the credit-funded portion is topped up to the merchant.
  • Card on file is required to apply credit, even if the credit fully covers the visit — patients with no card on file see the redemption surface but with the control disabled and copy directing them to add a card.
  • Loyalty credit cannot be applied to pay-at-office visits, post-no-show punitive charges, or other non-visit charges. The BNPL upfront no-show-fee freeze is a valid redemption target — see below.

BNPL redemption mechanic

Credit reduces the upfront no-show-fee freeze

On a BNPL booking with applied credit, the credit reduces the upfront no-show-fee freeze placed at T-24h (not the remaining-amount freeze placed on visit day). The patient sees a correspondingly smaller hold at T-24h — immediate confirmation that the credit landed. Once that freeze is placed, the credit is consumed regardless of visit outcome (visit happens, late cancel, or no-show). Cancellations in the free window — before T-24h — restore the credit to the patient's balance.

Receipts (transparency at checkout)

  • The booking success page displays the breakdown: service price, loyalty credit applied, amount charged or to-be-charged to card.
  • A supplementary receipt PDF is generated post-capture — for upfront-paid visits at checkout, for BNPL visits after the post-visit capture fires.
  • The same PDF is offered for download across four surfaces: web booking success page, web "My Visits", the patient app's Payments / Transactions section, and as an email attachment on the existing booking-confirmation / charge-completion email.
  • Localised to Spanish (es-MX) and Portuguese (pt-BR) at launch. Structured so IT-style tax-evidence fields can be added later without re-architecting.

Marketplace search filter

A new filter on the marketplace search results page lets patients narrow to doctors and facilities that accept online payment via DP Payments — so a patient with a balance can immediately see where their credit is usable. The filter is exposable as a shareable deeplink (notifications, support copy, expiry reminders).

Credit Lifecycle

1 · Granted

Credit lands on the patient's balance after the qualifying visit closes its payment cycle. From this moment it is the patient's property — DP cannot reclaim it except through the fraud admin tool.

2a · Redeemed

Patient applies some or all of the balance at checkout on a future online-paid visit with an MX/BR DP Payments doctor. Doctor still receives 100% of the visit price via a Platform→merchant top-up at payout time.

2b · Expired

Any unspent portion of a grant expires six months after the grant date — each grant has its own expiry timer, redemption of one grant does not extend another. At expiry the unspent portion is removed and the patient is notified. The expired amount is recorded as breakage for finance.

2c · Revoked (fraud)

An internal admin tool can revoke a specific grant manually after detected abuse — this is the only path that removes granted credit. The same tool also handles legitimate-non-fraud manual unwinds (e.g. restoring credit redeemed on a chargebacked visit).

Invariant: no clawback after grant

Once a credit has been granted, any subsequent refund, chargeback, or cancellation of the qualifying visit does not remove the credit from the patient's balance. The balance never goes negative. This is what protects the program from "book → grant → refund → spend" abuse without needing a pre-grant delay timer; it also makes the credit a real promotional gift in legal/T&Cs terms (which is what keeps the tax treatment clean).

Money Flow at Redemption

The doctor must remain at 100% of the visit price on every redeeming visit — no temporary short-funding, no waiting for top-up. The credit-funded portion comes from DocPlanner's pre-funded platform account at dLocal and is settled into the doctor's merchant account at payout time.

The Platform→merchant top-up uses dLocal's create-transfer-v2-platforms primitive. It is deferred to payout time (not redemption time) so a later visit cancellation does not incur a round-trip transfer fee.

Chargeback semantics

A chargeback on a redeeming visit reverses only the Platform→merchant top-up associated with that redemption — the credit-funded portion is pulled from the merchant's settlement balance first, falling to the Platform balance if insufficient. The patient's loyalty balance is unaffected — redeemed credit stays redeemed. Legitimate-non-fraud chargebacks can be unwound manually via the Collection admin tool.

EU readiness from day one

A per-market cumulative credit-funded transaction value is tracked and exposed in reporting. MX and BR don't gate on this today, but the accumulator and reporting hook exist from launch so a future EU rollout can monitor against the EUR 1M per-market regulatory threshold without re-platforming.

Experiment design

  • A/B 50/50 treatment vs. control per market, running in parallel in MX and BR.
  • Treatment population: loyalty offer visible in the booking flow, qualifying card-saves earn the grant, redemption surface visible at checkout for patients with a non-zero balance.
  • Control population: no loyalty offer surfaced, no grant earned, no redemption surface.
  • Per-country safety toggle: each market can be paused independently if metrics or bug reports indicate a problem in that market only.
  • Primary KPIs: online-paid share, wallet-adoption rate (cards on file), short-term retention (rebooking inside the credit's 6-month lifetime).

Patient-visible launch ordering

Collection ships first to land the credit ledger schema and the grant pipeline. Redemption ships second. The A/B assignment is wired in Collection but kept dormant (no treatment patients see the promo, no grants fire) until Redemption is also live behind a single shared feature flag. The flag flip — patient-visible launch — happens once both sub-cycles are merged and dLocal sandbox tests on the redemption funds-flow side have passed.

  • Loyalty across product lines beyond marketplace patient bookings

Common Questions

Anticipated questions from stakeholders — finance, legal, doctors-side, and operations.

Why patient-side credit and not a doctor-side discount?

We ranked three architectures with our MX finance counterpart:

  1. Best — patient-side credit (what we are building). DP funds the reward; the patient owns the credit; the doctor is invisible to the program and receives 100% of the visit price as on any non-loyalty visit. SAT (the Mexican tax authority) sees the patient paying the full service price out of their own funds (card + credit). Clean on every side.
  2. OK — doctor-side reward with a B2B subsidy invoice. The doctor would invoice DP per transaction for a "marketing subsidy". This is operationally non-viable: every doctor would need to opt in and to issue extra paperwork, and most practitioners are IVA-exempt on medical services but a "marketing subsidy" invoice would trigger IVA they don't otherwise pay. Rational doctors would opt out. No program at scale.
  3. Bad — doctor-funded but framed as a patient gift in T&Cs. Creates a three-way mismatch with SAT (doctor issues a CFDI for less than the patient paid, but the patient owns a credit they didn't pay for). Ruled out.

Patient-side credit is also the cleanest architectural analogue to platforms that already do this at scale: Amazon's "Discount Provided by Amazon" line, Allegro's AlleDiscount, and the Starbucks gift-card model (platform-funded float, customer owns the credit, recognised as deferred expense until redemption).

Why one-time and not recurring earning?

The qualifying action we want to pay for is saving a card on file. A patient does that once. Anything beyond the first grant pays for behaviour we get for free (paying online with an already-saved card) instead of behaviour we are trying to change.

The data model and grant trigger are designed so a future repeat-earning rule can be turned on without a schema migration or T&Cs rewrite — but the launch is deliberately narrow so we can read the experiment cleanly.

Why does the doctor get paid the full visit price on a redeeming visit? Where does the money come from?

Doctor invisibility is a hard invariant. On a redeeming visit, the patient's card is charged only for the remainder (visit price − credit applied); the credit-funded portion is transferred from DocPlanner's pre-funded platform account at dLocal into the doctor's merchant account at payout time, using dLocal's create-transfer-v2-platforms primitive. The doctor's settlement, dashboards, invoices and tax documents are identical to a non-loyalty visit. They never see the program.

We defer the top-up to payout time (rather than firing it at the moment of redemption) so a later cancellation of the visit does not incur a round-trip transfer fee.

What if a patient gets a refund after the credit is granted? Or charges back the qualifying visit?

The credit stays on the patient's balance. No clawback after grant. The same applies to redeeming visits: a chargeback reverses only the Platform→merchant top-up, never the patient's credit position.

This isn't generosity — it's load-bearing for the program's legal and tax framing. The credit is the patient's property from the moment it's granted; T&Cs explicitly state it as non-revocable except for fraud. That is what lets us book it as a liability (deferred expense) rather than a per-transaction marketing expense, and what lets SAT recognise the patient as having paid the full service price from their own funds (card + credit).

The "book → grant → refund → spend" abuse vector is bounded by: (a) one-time per patient, (b) six-month expiry, (c) per-market credit value is a small fraction of an average visit, (d) the fraud admin tool as a kill switch if a pattern emerges.

Can the patient cash out the credit, transfer it, or use it outside DocPlanner?

No, no, and no — and the T&Cs say so explicitly. The credit is non-cashable, non-transferable between patients, and redeemable only on bookings paid through DP Payments. It also expires on patient account closure (in addition to the per-grant six-month timer). This is what keeps the program non-safeguarded — not a regulated financial product, no cash value.

Patient-facing copy uses "credit" / "wallet credit" / "voucher". The word "cashback" is forbidden on every patient-facing surface — even though the mental model people fall into, hearing about "a reward for paying online", is exactly cashback-to-card. Correct it explicitly in stakeholder comms.

How does the patient redeem on a BNPL booking? Doesn't BNPL not charge anything upfront?

On BNPL, the credit reduces the upfront no-show-fee freeze — the authorisation placed on the patient's card at T-24h, when the late-cancellation window begins. The patient sees a smaller hold than they otherwise would. That is the redemption.

Once the no-show-fee freeze has been placed, the credit is consumed regardless of visit outcome — visit happens (capture proceeds at the original NSF value, with the Platform→merchant top-up making up the credit-funded portion), late cancel (same), or no-show (same). Only a cancellation in the free window, before the freeze is placed at T-24h, restores the credit to the patient's balance.

The trade-off: DP subsidises the no-show fee on actual no-shows. The cost is bounded by the per-market credit value times no-show-rate-on-redeeming-bookings; the win is that the redemption is locked in early — at T-24h, while the patient's card is freshly added — which is a UX-trust beat we don't get if the redemption only materialises at post-visit capture.

When exactly does the credit actually appear on the patient's balance?

It depends on how they paid for the qualifying visit:

  • Upfront-paid visit: after visit.endAt has passed. A scheduled job sweeps visits past their endAt and fires the grant — provided no cancellation or refund has been recorded against the qualifying payment at the moment the job evaluates eligibility. Reschedules push the evaluation moment to the new endAt.
  • BNPL / deferred / post-visit-capture: at the same operational moment as the existing post-visit capture for that flow — e.g. the 48-hour cron, or earlier if the doctor marks the visit outcome and that closes the payment cycle.

Both flows produce a single audit record per grant (patient, doctor, country, visit, payment, amount, currency, grant date, expiry date). The patient receives an in-product + email notification at the moment the grant fires.

What happens if the patient removes their last card? Or never adds one?

The balance is preserved but cannot be redeemed until a card is added back. At checkout, patients without a card see the redemption surface with the control disabled and copy telling them to add a card. The card-required guard applies even when the credit fully covers the visit — we never let a visit complete without a card on file, so we always have a tail-risk fallback (e.g. for chargebacks on the Platform→merchant top-up).

If a patient never adds a card back and the grant sits on the balance for six months, it expires as breakage. That is consistent with the program's design: the credit is real money owed to the patient (deferred expense) for the period it exists; breakage on expiry is policy, not a bug.

What is the financial trail? How does DocPlanner book this in accounting?

Three layers:

  1. At grant. Each granted credit is booked as a liability (deferred expense / amount owed to the patient) on DocPlanner's balance sheet, NOT as a marketing expense at the moment of grant. The credit is the patient's property; the obligation lives on our books until it is redeemed or expires.
  2. At redemption or expiry. The liability is released — into a real expense (on redemption, when DP funds the Platform→merchant top-up) or into breakage income (on expiry, when the unspent portion is recognised as no-longer-owed).
  3. Tax deductibility. dLocal periodically (monthly) invoices DocPlanner for "loyalty program services" — that locally tax-valid invoice is what makes the underlying spend deductible per market. This is being worked out with dLocal pricing (Costanza Besozzi) and DP Head of Finance; it is not part of the engineering cycle scope.

Audit-grade records exist from day one for every grant, redemption, and expiry, linked to the underlying payment and visit identifiers — so finance can reconcile against dLocal payment records during monthly close.

What about EU rollout? Doesn't the EU have caps on programs like this?

The EU's e-money / safeguarding regime caps platform-funded credit programs at EUR 1M per market before they cross into regulated territory. MX and BR don't gate on this, but a future EU rollout will — so the architecture tracks per-market cumulative credit-funded transaction value from day one and exposes it in reporting. No re-platforming needed when we expand into PL / IT / ES; we just start reading the accumulator.

How can we be sure the doctor never sees the program?

Doctor invisibility is a cross-cycle invariant that gets verified by spot-check across every doctor-facing surface during the Redemption sub-cycle — settings, transaction list, payouts, doctor app, doctor email. No surface discloses or references the program. The doctor's CFDI / NFS-e, payout, and any tax document are identical to a non-loyalty visit.

This is non-negotiable for two reasons: (a) operational — the goal is loyalty running transparently in the background, not selling extra product to each merchant; (b) tax — the moment a doctor's invoicing touches the program, the patient-side architecture stops being clean and we slide into the doctor-side options that were ruled out.
