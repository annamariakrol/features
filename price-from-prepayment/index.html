Price From Prepayment

Enable online payments for variable-price services — patients pay a booking deposit (minimum price), settle the rest at the office.

Value Proposition for Doctors

Fewer no-shows, more protected time

Patients have financial commitment before the visit. Variable-price services get the same protection as fixed-price ones.

Guaranteed minimum revenue per visit

At least the minimum price is collected before the patient walks in. The rest is settled at the office, as usual.

No extra effort required

The feature activates automatically for existing variable-price services. No reconfiguration, no new setup. Patients see clear messaging. For admin bookings, a simple toggle gives per-booking control.

How It Works

Patient Booking (User)

  • Patient selects a "price from" service and sees: "from {min_price}"
  • Payment flow collects the minimum price as a booking deposit (upfront or BNPL card-save)
  • Clear messaging: "You'll pay the minimum price online. The final price depends on the consultation — any difference will be settled at the specialist's office."
  • All notifications (SMS, WhatsApp, email, push) include "price may vary" messaging

Admin Booking (Doctor)

  • Doctor sees a toggle/checkbox for "price from" services on the booking form
  • Toggle ON: uses minimum price with "price may vary" messaging
  • Toggle OFF: doctor enters a fixed price, standard notifications

BNPL (Book Now Pay Later)

  • No-show fee = 100% of minimum price (no split into separate transactions)
  • Booking steps display all payment rules + "minimum price / final price may vary" messaging

Additional Benefit: Search Badge Loophole Fix

Doctors and facilities with the "online payments" badge in search results are currently boosted even if none of their services can actually be paid online. A key driver of this loophole is "price from" services — they're excluded from the payment flow, but the badge is determined at the address level, not the service level.

This feature directly fixes the price-from portion of that loophole by making those services payable.

Definitions

Fully fixed: ALL services on the address are "price from" with a valid minimum price — once the deposit model is enabled, every service is payable and the badge is 100% accurate.
Improved: The address has a mix of "price from" services (now payable) and services with no price at all (still not payable) — the badge becomes partially accurate.

Scope

In Scope

  • Enable payment flow for "price from" user bookings
  • Booking success page shows payment info
  • Wallet pages show "from {min_price}" with explanation
  • Minimum price as charged amount (upfront + BNPL)
  • BNPL no-show fee override to 100% of minimum
  • All notification channels updated with "price may vary"
  • Booking flow copy in all 6 locales

  • Remove platform restrictions for "price from" schedules
  • Doctor context in visit details drawer
  • Feature gate for controlled rollout
  • Mixed bookings (sum fixed + minimums)
  • Native mobile app parity

Out of Scope

  • Collecting remaining difference online (Phase 2)
  • Doctor dashboard changes beyond context + toggle
  • Changes to "price from" configuration at service level
  • Retroactive changes to existing bookings
  • Automated refund when final < minimum (Phase 2)

Edge Cases

  • Mixed bookings: Sum fixed + minimums — payment flow not blocked
  • Zero minimum: Excluded from payment flow (cannot charge 0)
  • Cancellation (upfront): Same refund rules as fixed-price
  • Cancellation (BNPL): Nothing charged in free window, standard BNPL rules apply
  • Wallet / saved cards: Same flow as fixed-price; patient always confirms
  • BNPL info: Booking steps show all rules + "price may vary" messaging
  • Reschedule: Deposit carries over
  • Final < minimum: Doctor initiates manual refund (no automation in Phase 1)
  • Admin toggle: Price-from ON = minimum + messaging; OFF = fixed price entered by doctor
  • Admin payment mode: Mandatory/optional per calendar settings, same as user
  • Doctor notification: Visit details drawer shows deposit context
  • Commission: On deposit (minimum price) only, standard invoice label

Common Questions

Real questions raised during design and stakeholder reviews, with data-backed answers.

Why can't doctors just set a fixed price?

For many specialties, the final scope of a visit genuinely cannot be known in advance. Real examples from our database:

  • Dental consultation (from 100 PLN): A check-up may stay a check-up, or it may become a filling, an X-ray, or a referral. Doctors write things like: "The price shown is approximate. The final cost will be determined after evaluation and may change depending on the scope of treatment."
  • Gynaecology (from 250 PLN): One doctor offers: consultation 250 PLN / + ultrasound + Pap smear 350 PLN / + LBC cytology 450 PLN / + HPV test 550 PLN. The patient books a "consultation" but the scope depends on what the doctor finds.
  • Root canal (from 450–950 PLN): Price depends on how many canals the tooth has (1–4), whether it's primary or retreatment, and whether a microscope is needed.

Rough split: ~40% of "price from" services are genuinely variable (dental, surgical, diagnostic). ~60% could probably be fixed prices if doctors configured them properly — but we can't force that change. Phase 1 unlocks payment for the legitimate cases and gives us data on the rest.

The "problem" varies dramatically by specialty — which is exactly why a blanket phase-1 test in Poland (a dental-heavy market) is the right approach.

Why call it a "booking deposit" and not "prepayment"? Doesn't the word "from" create a price anchoring problem?

Both points are valid. "Prepayment" implies full payment — a patient who pays a "prepayment" expects the price to be settled. "Deposit" signals partial payment — most people know a deposit is just a portion of the total. We changed all patient-facing copy to "booking deposit" before the request was finalised.

On price anchoring: yes, showing "from 100 EUR" makes 100 the mental reference point, and anything above feels like a penalty. The ideal fix — showing typical ranges like "Most patients pay 280–420 EUR" — requires data we don't have. We have minimum prices; we don't track what patients actually pay at the office (that money goes directly to the doctor).

What we can do in the design:

  • Frame the charged amount as a deposit, not a price: "Booking deposit: 150 PLN"
  • Add a clear disclaimer on the payment screen: "The final cost depends on the scope of the consultation. Any difference is settled at the specialist's office."

The word "deposit" already does heavy lifting — it tells patients "this is a part of the cost" rather than "this is the cost." Phase 1 will tell us whether that framing is enough.

Won't patients feel financially trapped if the final price is much higher?

The "contractor" analogy — paying 2k upfront with no idea of the final bill — is vivid but slightly off. A closer analogy: you call a plumber for a leaky faucet. They charge a 100 EUR call-out fee (the deposit you pay when booking). When they arrive, they assess the problem — it might be a 20-minute fix (total: 100 EUR, your deposit covers it) or a pipe replacement (total: 350 EUR, you pay the extra 250 after). You know the minimum upfront, the plumber explains the scope when they see the problem, and you choose whether to proceed.

Two important nuances:

  • The "surprise" typically isn't within the same visit. For dental — the most common case — a patient books a consultation at ~150 PLN and the doctor examines them. If a root canal is needed, it's scheduled as a separate booking at a different price. The patient leaves the consultation having paid exactly the deposit, with a treatment plan and cost estimate for what comes next.
  • Today there is zero online payment for these services. The patient books for free, shows up, and pays whatever the doctor says at the desk. We're not introducing price uncertainty — it already exists. We're adding a minimum commitment with explicit messaging, which is strictly more transparent than the current experience.
Open design question The copy on the payment screen and wallet pages is where this is won or lost. The disclaimer — "The final price depends on the scope of the consultation" — needs to be prominent, not buried in small print.
Are we fixing a symptom? Shouldn't we fix how doctors configure their services instead?

Honest answer: yes, partly. Many doctors use "price from" because they'd rather show a low price than risk scaring patients away — not because the service is genuinely variable. That's a configuration problem.

But also no — there are legitimate medical reasons for variable pricing, and we can't force doctors to reconfigure thousands of services before we ship. What we can do:

  • Short-term (Phase 1): Unlock payment for the legitimate cases and measure what happens.
  • Medium-term: Add a nudge when doctors enable the deposit toggle — prompt them to fill in their service description if it's empty. That's where they should set patient expectations.
  • Long-term: Phase 1 data will tell us whether conversion is low because the deposit model doesn't work, or low because certain service types genuinely shouldn't use it — which would give us a data-backed argument for better service configuration.

We're not building this instead of fixing the root cause. We're building it to understand the problem better.

What's our conversion target, and how long until we know if it's working?

We can't measure this directly yet because "price from" services are blocked from online payments today — there's no baseline. We're building this feature precisely to create that baseline.

Target: 15–20% user booking conversion in Phase 1 (Poland). For reference, fixed-price upfront conversion in Poland is ~47%. We're setting a conservative target because variable pricing is inherently more friction than a fixed price.

Key post-launch signals we'll watch:

  • Payment-step drop-off rate: price-from vs. fixed-price
  • Booking volume before vs. after (does adding a payment barrier reduce total bookings?)
  • Net revenue impact: value of any lost bookings vs. GMV from deposits collected
  • Admin toggle adoption: how often doctors override to a fixed price (high override rate = deposit model isn't landing)

Phase 2 — collecting the remaining amount online — is contingent on Phase 1 results. If conversion is too low, we iterate on copy and design. If booking volume drops, we reconsider the rollout approach.
